Economic Development Directorate

Proposed Development Contribution Scheme 2017-2021

Circulated, (i) report of the Director, Economic Development and Planning, dated

20th December, 2016, (ii) Chief Executive’s Report on submissions received dated 20th

December, 2016, and (iii) copy of Draft Scheme.

Councillor Gleeson stated that the scheme was the first Joint Contribution Scheme

for the City and County since the amalgamation of the Authorities in 2014. He said that it

aimed to strike a balance between the funding of public infrastructure and the need to

encourage economic activity and promote sustainable development patterns. He added that

it was clear that there were areas of great divergence and complexity.

He stated that the challenge in developing the new scheme had been to try and

address the issues facing the City and the County in something that could make a difference

in where development would take place for the common good. Those issues included

addressing the shortage of housing stock, addressing dereliction in protected structures

through incentivising reuse and promoting the development of economic activity through

providing reduced charges for the likes of office development in the City Centre. He said

that the scheme also promoted, through incentives, energy efficiencies in the Council’s new

buildings. He said that the preparation work on the scheme had constantly kept an eye to

direction from Government in relation to promoting and supporting economic recovery. At

all stages, the review had worked within the parameters set by the Development

Contribution Guidelines for Planning Authorities issued in January 2013.

Throughout the process, the Elected Members had been involved and had met on 5

different occasions prior to that day’s meeting. He said that the proposal before Members

had gone through an extensive consultation process with a wide variety of stakeholders.

The business in hand was consideration of the Chief Executive’s Report and the Draft

Scheme.

13.

Question submitted by Councillor C. Prendiville

I will ask at the next Meeting for the details of the quality and nature of the new

emergency homeless accommodation provided in Limerick before Christmas, in terms of

the privacy, washing facilities, type of beds, cleanliness and length of stay.

REPLY:

The Winter Beds Initiative is a service provided by Limerick City and County

Council through the Limerick Homeless Action Team, managed and delivered

by Novas Initiatives, a provider of homeless services with longstanding

expertise, professionalism and experience. The service provides beds to

people at risk of rough sleeping. The service is for up to 14 people, with extra

provision in exceptional circumstances for a further 6 people.

Rooms are shared, with the possibility of single room provision where

appropriate. There are 5 full bathrooms on site, with showering facilities for

clients. Laundry facilities are also provided for clients. All beds and bedding

in the facility were purchased new. Bed linen is washed daily. A professional

cleaning service cleans the facility daily. Beds are allocated to clients on a

night-by-night basis.
